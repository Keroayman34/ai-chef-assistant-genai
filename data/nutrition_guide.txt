COMPREHENSIVE NUTRITION GUIDE

MACRONUTRIENTS OVERVIEW
======================

Proteins:
- Essential for muscle repair and immune function
- Sources: chicken, turkey, fish, lean beef, eggs, beans, tofu, lentils, greek yogurt
- Recommended: 0.7-1.0g per lb of body weight for active individuals
- Amino acids: contains all 9 essential amino acids in complete proteins

Carbohydrates:
- Primary energy source for your body and brain
- Complex carbs (slow-digesting): oats, brown rice, sweet potatoes, whole wheat, legumes
- Simple carbs (quick energy): fruits, honey, white rice (use strategically)
- Fiber: 25-35g daily for digestive health
- Choose complex carbs for sustained energy and better satiety

Fats:
- Essential for hormone production and nutrient absorption
- Unsaturated fats (healthy): olive oil, avocados, nuts, seeds, fatty fish
- Saturated fats: limit to 10% of calories
- Trans fats: avoid completely
- Omega-3 fatty acids: salmon, sardines, walnuts, flaxseeds

CALORIE BASICS
==============
- 1g protein = 4 calories
- 1g carbohydrates = 4 calories
- 1g fat = 9 calories
- 1g alcohol = 7 calories

Daily caloric needs vary by:
- Age, sex, height, weight
- Activity level (sedentary, lightly active, moderately active, very active)
- Metabolism rate

BALANCED MEAL STRUCTURE
======================

A balanced plate contains:
1. Protein (25-35% of plate): palm-sized portion
2. Whole grains or complex carbs (40-50% of plate): fist-sized portion
3. Vegetables (25-35% of plate): 2 fist-sized portions
4. Healthy fat (10-15% of plate): thumb-sized portion

Example ratios:
- 40% carbs, 30% protein, 30% fat (typical balanced)
- 30% carbs, 40% protein, 30% fat (higher protein for muscle building)
- 50% carbs, 25% protein, 25% fat (endurance athletes)

NUTRIENT TIMING
===============

Pre-workout (1-2 hours before):
- Complex carbs + moderate protein
- Example: oatmeal with banana and almonds
- Avoid high fat and fiber (can cause discomfort)

Post-workout (within 30-60 minutes):
- Fast-digesting carbs + protein
- Example: chocolate milk, protein shake with fruit
- Goal: replenish glycogen and start muscle repair

MICRONUTRIENTS (VITAMINS & MINERALS)
====================================

Iron:
- Sources: red meat, spinach, lentils, fortified cereals
- Deficiency: fatigue, weakness, poor immunity
- Absorption: improved with Vitamin C

Calcium:
- Sources: dairy, fortified plant milks, leafy greens, sardines
- Daily need: 1000-1200mg
- Function: bone health, muscle contraction, nerve transmission

Vitamin D:
- Sources: fatty fish, egg yolks, mushrooms, sunlight
- Function: calcium absorption, immune function, mood regulation
- Many people are deficient, especially in winter

Magnesium:
- Sources: pumpkin seeds, almonds, spinach, dark chocolate
- Deficiency can cause: muscle cramps, poor sleep, anxiety
- Daily need: 310-420mg

Potassium:
- Sources: bananas, sweet potatoes, avocados, spinach
- Function: muscle contraction, blood pressure regulation
- Works with sodium for hydration

HYDRATION GUIDELINES
====================

Water intake:
- General rule: 0.5-1 oz per lb of body weight
- Increases with activity level and climate
- Pale yellow urine = well hydrated
- Dark yellow = drink more water

Electrolytes (sodium, potassium, magnesium):
- Important during intense or prolonged exercise
- Sports drinks useful only for 60+ minutes of activity
- Can be obtained from whole foods: bananas, coconut water, salted nuts

HEALTHY EATING PRINCIPLES
=========================

1. Whole foods over processed
2. Adequate protein with every meal
3. Plenty of vegetables (aim for variety and color)
4. Whole grains instead of refined grains
5. Healthy fats daily
6. Limit added sugars (target: less than 25g daily for women, 36g for men)
7. Consistent meal timing for energy
8. Mindful eating (chew slowly, pay attention)
9. Cook at home more often
10. Stay hydrated throughout the day

COMMON NUTRITION MISTAKES
=======================

1. Skipping meals (leads to overeating later)
2. Not eating enough protein (loss of muscle mass)
3. Eating too many calories "healthy" foods (nuts, olive oil, avocados are calorie-dense)
4. Ignoring portion sizes
5. Drinking calories (juice, sugary drinks, alcohol)
6. Fad diets (unsustainable and often unhealthy)
7. Not reading nutrition labels
8. Extreme restriction (leads to burnout and binge eating)
9. Comparing your needs to others
10. Ignoring sleep and stress (affects food choices and metabolism)

FOOD STORAGE & SAFETY
====================

Refrigerate:
- Meat, poultry, fish: 1-2 days
- Leftovers: 3-4 days
- Eggs: 3-4 weeks
- Vegetables: varies (typically 1-2 weeks)

Freeze:
- Meat: 3-4 months
- Fish: 2-3 months
- Vegetables: 8-12 months
- Cooked meals: 3-4 months

Keep room temperature:
- Dry pasta, rice, oats
- Canned goods
- Nuts, seeds (or refrigerate to extend shelf life)
- Whole fruits

This guide provides general nutrition information. Individual needs vary based on health conditions, allergies, and goals. Consult healthcare professionals for personalized advice.
