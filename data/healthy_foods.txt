HEALTHY FOODS REFERENCE

LEAN PROTEINS (Low Calorie, High Protein)
========================================

Poultry:
- Chicken breast: 165 cal, 31g protein per 100g (skinless)
- Turkey breast: 135 cal, 29g protein per 100g
- Best for: meal prep, grilling, versatile in recipes

Fish & Seafood:
- Salmon: 208 cal, 20g protein per 100g (plus omega-3 benefits)
- Tuna: 132 cal, 29g protein per 100g (canned in water)
- Tilapia: 96 cal, 20g protein per 100g (lean white fish)
- Shrimp: 99 cal, 24g protein per 100g (low fat)
- Sardines: packed with omega-3 and calcium

Beef & Pork:
- Lean ground beef (90%): 180 cal, 19g protein per 100g
- Sirloin steak: 180 cal, 26g protein per 100g
- Pork tenderloin: 143 cal, 27g protein per 100g
- Choose lean cuts, trim visible fat

Eggs & Dairy:
- Eggs: 155 cal, 13g protein per 100g (whole egg)
- Egg whites: 52 cal, 11g protein per 100g
- Greek yogurt (nonfat): 59 cal, 10g protein per 100g
- Cottage cheese (low-fat): 72 cal, 11g protein per 100g
- Milk (skim): 35 cal, 3.4g protein per 100ml

Plant-Based Proteins:
- Lentils: 116 cal, 9g protein per cooked cup
- Black beans: 227 cal, 15g protein per cooked cup
- Chickpeas: 269 cal, 15g protein per cooked cup
- Tofu: 76 cal, 8g protein per 100g
- Tempeh: 193 cal, 19g protein per 100g
- Edamame: 95 cal, 11g protein per 100g

COMPLEX CARBOHYDRATES (Slow Energy, High Fiber)
===============================================

Grains:
- Oatmeal: 150 cal, 5g protein, 27g carbs per cup cooked
- Brown rice: 108 cal, 2.5g protein, 23g carbs per cup cooked
- Quinoa: 222 cal, 8g protein, 39g carbs per cup cooked (complete protein)
- Whole wheat bread: 100 cal, 4g protein per slice
- Sweet potato: 103 cal, 2g protein, 24g carbs per medium
- Barley: 193 cal, 3.5g protein, 44g carbs per cup cooked

Legumes (Protein + Carbs):
- All beans are excellent: high fiber, high protein
- Lentils: especially high protein (9g per cup cooked)
- Peas: 125 cal, 9g protein per cup cooked

Vegetables (Very Low Calorie, High Nutrients):
- Broccoli: 34 cal, 3.7g protein, 7g carbs per cup
- Spinach: 7 cal, 1g protein per cup raw
- Bell peppers: 30 cal, 1g protein per cup
- Carrots: 25 cal per cup, good source of beta-carotene
- Brussels sprouts: 38 cal, 3g protein per cup
- Kale: 33 cal, 3.3g protein per cup (superfood)
- Broccoli, cauliflower, zucchini: very low calorie, filling
- Dark leafy greens: spinach, kale, arugula (nutrient-dense)

Fruits (Carbs + Fiber + Vitamins):
- Berries: low glycemic, high antioxidants
  * Blueberries: 57 cal, 14g carbs per cup
  * Strawberries: 49 cal, 12g carbs per cup
  * Raspberries: 64 cal, 12g fiber per cup (very high fiber!)
- Bananas: 89 cal, 23g carbs per medium (good potassium)
- Apples: 95 cal, 25g carbs per medium (skin has fiber)
- Oranges: 62 cal, 16g carbs per medium
- Watermelon: 46 cal, 11g carbs per cup (very hydrating)

HEALTHY FATS (Calorie-Dense, Essential)
======================================

Nuts & Seeds:
- Almonds: 164 cal, 6g protein per ounce (14 nuts)
- Walnuts: 185 cal, 4.3g protein per ounce (7 halves)
- Peanut butter: 94 cal, 4g protein per tablespoon (natural, unsweetened)
- Chia seeds: 138 cal, 5g protein per ounce (2 tbsp) - high fiber, omega-3
- Flaxseeds: 150 cal, 5g protein per ounce - plant-based omega-3
- Hemp seeds: 162 cal, 9.2g protein per ounce - complete protein
- Sunflower seeds: 165 cal, 5.5g protein per ounce

Oils & Fats:
- Extra virgin olive oil: 120 cal, 14g fat per tablespoon (anti-inflammatory)
- Coconut oil: 117 cal, 14g fat per tablespoon (MCT fats for energy)
- Avocado oil: 120 cal, 14g fat per tablespoon (high smoke point)
- Avocado: 160 cal, 3g protein, 9g fat per half (whole food source)

Fatty Fish:
- Salmon: 208 cal, 20g protein, omega-3 fatty acids
- Sardines: small fish, eat whole (including bones for calcium)
- Mackerel: oily fish, high omega-3
- Anchovies: calorie-efficient omega-3 source

SUPERFOODS (Extra Nutrient-Dense)
================================

Berries:
- High in antioxidants, low glycemic
- Can be fresh or frozen (just as nutritious)
- Versatile: smoothies, oatmeal, yogurt

Leafy Greens:
- Spinach: high iron, calcium, magnesium, folate
- Kale: vitamin K (bone health), vitamin C, antioxidants
- Arugula: low calorie, peppery flavor, sulforaphane (anti-cancer)
- Collard greens: similar benefits to kale

Cruciferous Vegetables:
- Broccoli, cauliflower, Brussels sprouts, cabbage
- High in sulforaphane (cancer-fighting compound)
- High in vitamin C and fiber

Fatty Fish:
- Omega-3 content reduces inflammation
- Supports heart, brain, and joint health
- Goal: 2-3 servings weekly

Fermented Foods:
- Yogurt: probiotics for gut health, high protein
- Kefir: more probiotics than yogurt
- Kimchi, sauerkraut: probiotics + enzymes
- Tempeh: fermented soy, easier to digest than tofu

============================

Breakfast Options:
- Oatmeal + berries + almonds + honey
- Scrambled eggs + whole wheat toast + avocado
- Greek yogurt + granola + berries
- Veggie omelet with spinach and mushrooms

Lunch Options:
- Grilled chicken + brown rice + steamed broccoli
- Tuna salad on whole wheat bread + side salad
- Lentil soup + whole grain bread
- Grilled salmon + sweet potato + asparagus

Dinner Options:
- Lean ground turkey + whole wheat pasta + marinara
- Baked white fish + quinoa + roasted vegetables
- Tofu stir-fry + brown rice + mixed vegetables
- Lean beef + roasted potatoes + salad

Snacks (Healthy):
- Apple + almond butter
- Greek yogurt + berries
- Handful of almonds
- Hard-boiled eggs
- String cheese + whole wheat crackers
- Hummus + vegetables

HYDRATION & BEVERAGES
====================

Best Choices:
- Water: zero calories, essential hydration
- Herbal tea: zero calories, various health benefits
- Green tea: antioxidants, mild caffeine
- Black coffee: zero calories, energy boost (no added sugar)
- Sparkling water: hydration with bubbles

Moderate (Occasional):
- Coffee with small amount of milk: acceptable
- Tea with honey: natural sweetener
- 100% fruit juice (small serving): high sugar despite nutrition
- Smoothies: calorie-controlled with whole foods

Limit or Avoid:
- Sugary drinks: soda, energy drinks, sweetened teas
- Commercial juices: high sugar, low fiber
- Alcohol: empty calories, affects judgment on food choices
- Sweetened coffee drinks: can be 300-500+ calories

SHOPPING TIPS FOR HEALTHY EATING
================================

Plan Before Shopping:
- Write a meal plan for the week
- Create organized shopping list by section
- Shop after eating (don't shop hungry!)

Read Labels:
- Nutrition facts: serving size, calories, macros
- Ingredients list: fewer ingredients = often better
- Watch for hidden sugars (sucrose, glucose, high fructose corn syrup)
- Look for: "whole grain", "high fiber", "low sodium"

Buy in Bulk:
- Frozen vegetables: just as nutritious, long shelf life, cost-effective
- Dried beans and lentils: cheap, shelf-stable
- Nuts in bulk: cheaper than packaged

Choose Quality:
- Fresh > Frozen > Canned (but frozen and canned are still healthy)
- Pasture-raised eggs when possible
- Wild-caught fish > farm-raised (usually)
- Organic produce for "Dirty Dozen" (high pesticide items)

Budget-Friendly Proteins:
- Eggs: cheapest protein source
- Beans and lentils: minimal cost
- Canned tuna/salmon: affordable omega-3
- Chicken thighs: cheaper than breast, more flavorful

This is a practical guide for everyday healthy eating. Individual dietary needs vary significantly. Always consult with a healthcare provider or registered dietitian for personalized nutrition advice, especially if you have medical conditions, allergies, or specific health goals.
